Process

  1. Inspect blade, note steel, geometry, and damage. Discuss goals (push-cut vs. toothy edge).
  2. Set bevels appropriately (water stones and/or belt system) while controlling heat to preserve temper.
  3. Repair chips and broken tips as needed, then refine through progressive grits.
  4. Deburr meticulously and finish (strop / ceramic) to your use-case.
  5. Final clean, oil, and safe return packaging.

About

Sharpen Sweet is a small, Tucson-based shop that believes sharp tools make cooking safer and more fun. Work is done carefully by hand and machine as appropriate''-stones for precision, belts for repair''-always with heat control and geometry in mind.

We serve home cooks, pros, and stylists. Drop-off by appointment preferred.
